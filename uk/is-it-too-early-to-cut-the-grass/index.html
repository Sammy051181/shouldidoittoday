UK grass and lawn weather

Is it too early to cut the grass?

It may be too early if the grass is still wet with dew, the ground is cold or soft, or the time is likely to disturb neighbours.

Check your local conditions

Short answer

Early mowing can be a problem even on a dry forecast day because grass often starts the morning damp.

Why the weather matters

This page helps with both meanings of “too early”: too early in the day and too early in the season.

Check my local grass weather

GO when...

  • Dew has dried.
  • The lawn is firm and not wet underfoot.
  • It is a reasonable time for noise.
  • The grass is actively growing.

CAUTION when...

  • It is early morning and the grass is damp.
  • Spring growth has only just started.
  • The ground is still cold or soft.
  • You are unsure about local noise expectations.

NO-GO when...

  • There is frost or ice.
  • The lawn is wet or muddy.
  • It is very early and likely to disturb people.
  • The grass is not growing or is dormant.

FAQs

Is it too early in the morning to cut grass?

If dew is still on the lawn or it is a very early noisy time, wait.

Is it too early in spring to cut grass?

Wait until grass is actively growing and conditions are mild and dry.

Can I cut grass at 7am?

It is often too early for dew and neighbour-friendly noise.

What is a good first mowing time?

Late morning or afternoon on a dry, mild day is usually better.

Does frost mean no cutting?

Yes. Avoid cutting frosty grass.
