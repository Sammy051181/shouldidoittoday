Quick answer

A good window-cleaning day is dry, not too windy, not freezing and unlikely to rain immediately afterwards.

GO when...

  • Rain risk is low for the next few hours.
  • Wind is manageable.
  • Temperatures are safely above freezing.
  • There is enough daylight.

CAUTION when...

  • Showers are possible later.
  • Wind may make ladder work or streak-free cleaning harder.
  • It is cold enough for surfaces to dry slowly.
  • Dust or pollen may return quickly.

NO-GO when...

  • Rain is likely soon.
  • Strong wind makes the job difficult or unsafe.
  • Freezing conditions could make surfaces slippery.
  • Storms are possible.

FAQs

Is it pointless to clean windows before rain?

Often yes. Rain can quickly leave marks, especially with wind and road dirt.

Can I clean windows in cold weather?

Avoid freezing conditions because water can ice over on surfaces or paths.

Is wind a problem for window cleaning?

Yes, wind can make equipment harder to control and may affect safety.

Is cloudy weather good for cleaning windows?

Cloudy but dry weather can be good because direct sun may dry water too quickly.
