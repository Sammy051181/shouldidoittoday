Quick answer

Most dogs can handle normal weather, but heat, storms, icy conditions and strong wind can change a routine walk into a poor choice.

GO when...

  • Temperature feels comfortable.
  • Rain risk is manageable.
  • Wind is not extreme.
  • There is no storm or dangerous heat risk.

CAUTION when...

  • It may be wet or windy.
  • It may be warm enough to shorten the walk.
  • Pavements may be hot in sunny weather.
  • You may need a coat, towel or different route.

NO-GO when...

  • It is too hot for a safe walk.
  • Storms, lightning or dangerous wind are possible.
  • Ice or snow may make paths unsafe.
  • Heavy rain makes visibility or road safety poor.

FAQs

When is it too hot to walk a dog?

If it feels hot to you, pavements may be hotter for paws. Shorten the walk, go early or late, or wait.

Can I walk my dog in the rain?

Light rain is often fine, but heavy rain, poor visibility and cold wind may make it unpleasant or unsafe.

Is wind dangerous for dog walks?

Strong wind can bring falling branches, debris and poor control near roads.

Should I avoid walking in storms?

Yes. Lightning and storm conditions are a NO-GO.
