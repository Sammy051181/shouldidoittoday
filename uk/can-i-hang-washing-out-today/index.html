Quick answer

Washing dries best when there is a dry spell, some airflow, enough daylight and manageable humidity. Sunshine helps, but wind and dry air can matter just as much.

GO when...

  • Several dry daylight hours are expected.
  • Rain risk is low.
  • There is enough breeze to help drying.
  • Humidity is not too high.

CAUTION when...

  • Cloudy weather may slow drying.
  • Humidity is high.
  • There is a small shower risk.
  • There may not be enough daylight left.

NO-GO when...

  • Rain is likely or already falling.
  • There are not enough dry hours left.
  • Humidity is very high and drying will be slow.
  • Strong wind could blow washing off the line.

FAQs

Can washing dry outside when it is cloudy?

Yes, washing can dry on cloudy days if the air is dry enough and there is some breeze.

Is wind better than sun for drying clothes?

Both help. Wind moves moist air away from clothes, while sunshine adds warmth.

Will clothes dry outside in winter?

They can, but drying is usually slower because there is less warmth and daylight.

What if there is a small chance of rain?

Use caution. If you cannot bring washing in quickly, it may be better to wait.
