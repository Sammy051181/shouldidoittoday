Can you cut grass in the rain?

Cutting grass in the rain is usually a bad idea.

Short answer

In most cases, do not cut grass while it is raining. Wait for the rain to stop and for the lawn to dry enough for a clean, safe cut.

Why the weather matters

Rain makes grass heavier, slipperier and harder to cut. It can also increase the chance of mower clogging and poor results.

GO when...

  • Rain has stopped, the grass is drying and the ground is firm.
  • You only need a light trim and conditions are improving.
  • You can cut later in a dry window.
  • The mower is suitable and the lawn is not slippery.

CAUTION when...

  • There is drizzle or light rain.
  • The grass is damp but not soaked.
  • The forecast is improving soon.
  • You are cutting a small flat area only.

NO-GO when...

  • It is raining steadily.
  • The lawn is slippery, muddy or waterlogged.
  • The mower clogs.
  • There is thunder, lightning or strong wind.

FAQs

Is it safe to cut grass in the rain?

It is usually better to avoid it because wet ground and poor visibility can increase risk.

Will rain damage the mower?

Wet grass can clog the mower and make it work harder.

Can I cut grass in drizzle?

Drizzle still leaves wet grass, so use caution and wait if possible.

Is it better to cut before or after rain?

Before rain can work if the grass is already dry and there is enough time.

What should I do if rain starts while mowing?

Stop if conditions become slippery, visibility drops or the mower starts clogging.
