Can you cut wet grass?

Wet grass is one of the clearest reasons to delay mowing.

Short answer

You can sometimes cut slightly damp grass if you have no other option, but it is usually better to wait until the grass blades and ground are dry.

Why the weather matters

Wet grass does not cut cleanly. It can bend rather than slice, clog the mower, leave clumps on the lawn and make the ground slippery or rutted.

GO when...

  • The grass is only lightly damp, not soaked.
  • The ground is firm underfoot.
  • No more rain is expected soon.
  • You can use a higher mower setting and go slowly.

CAUTION when...

  • The lawn is damp from dew or light rain.
  • The mower is struggling or leaving clumps.
  • The ground is soft in places.
  • There is more rain later.

NO-GO when...

  • Water squelches underfoot.
  • Grass clumps badly behind the mower.
  • The lawn is slippery or muddy.
  • Heavy rain has fallen recently or more rain is due.

FAQs

Why is cutting wet grass bad?

Wet grass clumps, tears more easily and can clog your mower.

Can I cut grass when it is damp?

Light dampness may be manageable, but wet grass and soft ground are a bad combination.

Will cutting wet grass damage the lawn?

It can damage the finish and may tear grass rather than cutting cleanly.

How long should I wait after rain?

Wait until the grass and the ground are dry enough that the mower does not leave clumps or marks.

Is wet grass dangerous to cut?

It can be more slippery, especially on slopes or uneven ground.
