Quick answer

A coat decision is not only about temperature. Wind, rain and how long you will be outside can make mild weather feel colder.

GO when...

  • Feels-like temperature is low.
  • Rain or showers are likely.
  • Wind makes it feel cooler.
  • You will be outside for a long period.

CAUTION when...

  • A light jacket may be useful.
  • Weather may change later.
  • It may be mild now but cooler in the evening.
  • A waterproof may be better than a warm coat.

NO-GO when...

  • It is warm, dry and calm enough.
  • You will only be outside briefly.
  • No rain is likely.
  • Evening temperatures stay mild.

FAQs

What temperature means I need a coat?

It depends on wind and personal preference, but lower feels-like temperatures make a coat more useful.

Do I need a waterproof today?

If rain risk is high, a waterproof may matter more than a warm coat.

Why does wind matter?

Wind can make the air feel colder and reduce comfort quickly.

Should I take a coat in the morning?

Check the whole day, especially if you will be out in the evening.

What if I feel the cold?

Use the checker as guidance, but adjust for your own comfort.
