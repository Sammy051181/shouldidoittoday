Quick answer

Running is usually fine in light weather, but heat, strong wind, icy conditions and storms can make it uncomfortable or unsafe.

GO when...

  • Temperature is comfortable for running.
  • Rain risk is manageable.
  • Wind is not too strong.
  • There is no storm, ice or heat concern.

CAUTION when...

  • It may be warm, wet or windy.
  • Surfaces may be slippery.
  • You may need to adapt pace, route or clothing.
  • Visibility may be reduced.

NO-GO when...

  • It is too hot, icy or stormy.
  • Strong wind could make exposed routes unsafe.
  • Heavy rain reduces visibility or footing.
  • There is lightning risk.

FAQs

Can I run in the rain?

Light rain can be fine, but heavy rain can reduce visibility and make surfaces slippery.

How hot is too hot to run?

Heat tolerance varies, but warm and humid conditions increase strain. Consider an easier pace or a cooler time.

Is wind bad for running?

Moderate wind is manageable, but strong gusts can make exposed routes difficult.

Should I run if it is icy?

Use caution or avoid it. Ice can make slips and falls much more likely.
