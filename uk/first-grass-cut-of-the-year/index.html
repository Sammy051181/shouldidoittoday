UK grass and lawn weather

First grass cut of the year

The first grass cut of the year should usually wait until the grass is actively growing and the lawn is dry enough.

Check your local conditions

Short answer

In the UK, the first cut often falls in spring, but weather matters more than the calendar.

Why the weather matters

The first cut should be light, careful and timed for a dry, mild spell.

Check my local grass weather

GO when...

  • Grass has started active growth.
  • The lawn is dry and firm.
  • There is no frost risk.
  • You can take a light first cut on a higher setting.

CAUTION when...

  • It is early spring and growth is uneven.
  • Recent rain has made the ground soft.
  • Cold nights are still likely.
  • The lawn is long but damp.

NO-GO when...

  • There is frost, ice or waterlogging.
  • The grass is dormant.
  • The ground is muddy.
  • Heavy rain or storms are expected.

FAQs

When is the first grass cut of the year in the UK?

Often in spring, but wait for mild, dry conditions and visible growth.

Can I cut grass in February?

Only in a mild dry spell if the grass is growing; often it is better to wait.

Can I cut grass in March?

Often yes if conditions are mild and dry enough.

How short should the first cut be?

Use a higher mower setting and avoid taking too much off.

Should I cut after winter rain?

Wait until the ground is firm and dry enough.
