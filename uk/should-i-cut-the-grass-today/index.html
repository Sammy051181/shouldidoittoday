Quick answer

A good grass-cutting day is usually dry, mild, not too windy and not immediately after heavy rain. Wet grass can clog the mower, tear the lawn and leave a poor finish.

GO when...

  • The grass is dry or drying well.
  • Rain risk is low for the next few hours.
  • Wind is manageable and it is still light enough.
  • Temperatures are mild enough for normal lawn growth.

CAUTION when...

  • There has been recent rain and the lawn may still be damp.
  • There is a small shower risk later.
  • It is getting late in the day.
  • It is winter or the grass may not be actively growing.

NO-GO when...

  • It is raining now or heavy rain is likely soon.
  • The lawn is wet underfoot.
  • There is strong wind, frost, ice or storm risk.
  • The ground is waterlogged.

FAQs

Can I cut grass after rain?

It is better to wait until the grass blades and ground have dried. Cutting wet grass can leave clumps and damage the lawn.

Is it bad to cut grass when it is windy?

Light wind is usually fine, but strong wind can make mowing messy and less safe, especially with debris or uneven ground.

What temperature is too cold to cut grass?

If the grass is not actively growing, or there is frost or ice risk, it is usually better to wait.

Should I cut grass in the evening?

You can, but avoid cutting too late when light is poor or the grass is becoming damp again.
