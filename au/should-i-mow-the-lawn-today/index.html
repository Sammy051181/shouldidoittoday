Quick answer

A good mowing day is dry enough, not too windy and not dangerously hot. Heat and storm risk are especially important.

GO when...

  • The lawn is dry enough.
  • Rain risk is low.
  • Wind is manageable.
  • Heat is not excessive.

CAUTION when...

  • It may be hot, so morning or evening may be better.
  • Recent rain may have left the lawn wet.
  • Wind or storms may develop.
  • Grass may be stressed in dry heat.

NO-GO when...

  • It is too hot for safe mowing.
  • Storms or strong wind are expected.
  • The lawn is wet or slippery.
  • Rain is likely soon.

FAQs

Should I mow in hot weather?

Avoid the hottest part of the day and use caution during heat.

Can I mow after rain?

Wait until the lawn and ground dry enough.

Is morning better for mowing?

Often yes in warm weather, if the grass is not still wet with dew.

Does wind matter?

Strong wind can make mowing harder and less safe.
