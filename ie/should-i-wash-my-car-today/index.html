Should I wash my car today in Ireland?

Check local rain risk and wind before washing the car — rain is the most common problem in Ireland.

Check your local conditions

GO when...

  • Rain risk is low for at least 2–3 hours.
  • Temperature is above freezing.
  • Wind is light or calm.
  • Dry spell forecast — the car will stay clean longer.

CAUTION when...

  • Showers possible later — the car may not stay clean.
  • Atlantic fronts can arrive quickly — check the hourly forecast.
  • High humidity slows drying.
  • Cold but dry — watch for frost overnight.

NO-GO when...

  • Rain is falling or likely within the next hour or two.
  • Gale or strong wind warnings are in place.
  • Frost or ice is forecast.
  • A front is approaching and the forecast is uncertain.

Frequently asked questions

Is there ever a good day to wash a car in Ireland?

Yes — high pressure in summer and early autumn can bring several dry days in a row. Check the hourly forecast for a window of 3–4 dry hours.

What temperature is too cold to wash a car in Ireland?

Below 0°C, water can freeze on door seals, locks and glass. Frost-prone winter mornings are best avoided.

How often should I wash my car in Ireland?

The wet climate and road salt in winter mean regular washing, particularly in the wheel arches and underside, helps prevent rust.

Can I use a jet wash in cold weather?

Yes, as long as temperatures are above freezing. Jet washing in freezing conditions risks ice forming on the car and on paths.
