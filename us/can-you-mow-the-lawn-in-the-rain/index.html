Can you mow the lawn in the rain?

Mowing in the rain is usually a bad idea.

Check your local conditions

Short answer

Wait until rain stops and the lawn has dried enough. Rain makes grass heavier, slipperier and harder to cut.

Why the weather matters

This page is for the direct rain decision and supports the wet grass and after-rain pages.

Check my local grass weather

GO when...

  • Rain has stopped and conditions are improving.
  • The lawn is only lightly damp.
  • The ground is firm.
  • You can wait for a better dry window.

CAUTION when...

  • There is drizzle.
  • Grass is damp but not soaked.
  • The area is flat and small.
  • The forecast improves soon.

NO-GO when...

  • It is actively raining.
  • The lawn is slippery or muddy.
  • The mower clogs.
  • Thunderstorms or lightning are possible.

FAQs

Is mowing in rain safe?

It is usually better to avoid it due to slipping, poor visibility and poor cutting conditions.

Can rain damage the mower?

Wet grass can clog the mower and make it work harder.

Can I mow in light drizzle?

It is still better to wait if possible.

What should I do if rain starts?

Stop if the lawn becomes slippery, visibility drops or the mower begins to clog.

Is mowing after rain better than during rain?

Yes, but only after the grass and soil have dried enough.
