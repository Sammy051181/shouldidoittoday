Can you mow the lawn after rain?

You can mow after rain only when the grass and soil are dry enough.

Check your local conditions

Short answer

Do not judge by the sky alone. Even after rain stops, the grass may still be too wet to cut properly.

Why the weather matters

This page targets the after-rain decision and links directly to the weather checker.

Check my local grass weather

GO when...

  • Rain has stopped and the lawn has dried.
  • Shoes do not get soaked walking on the grass.
  • The mower cuts without clumping.
  • No more rain is likely soon.

CAUTION when...

  • The top of the grass is drying but the base is damp.
  • The soil is firm in some areas but soft in others.
  • There are possible showers later.
  • You may need a higher mower setting.

NO-GO when...

  • The lawn is wet underfoot.
  • The mower clogs or leaves tracks.
  • Heavy rain fell recently.
  • More rain or storms are expected.

FAQs

How soon after rain can I mow?

There is no fixed time. Wait until the grass and soil are dry enough.

Can I mow after light rain?

Possibly, if the lawn dries quickly and the ground is firm.

Can I mow after heavy rain?

Usually not immediately. Heavy rain often leaves the lawn too wet or soft.

Should I mow before rain?

Only if the lawn is dry and rain is not imminent.

What if the lawn is partly wet?

Avoid wet areas or wait until the whole lawn is ready.
