Can you mow wet grass?

Wet grass is usually a reason to wait.

Check your local conditions

Short answer

You can sometimes mow lightly damp grass, but wet grass usually clumps, clogs the mower and cuts poorly.

Why the weather matters

This is one of the strongest lawn search clusters because people often need a practical yes/no after rain or morning dew.

Check my local grass weather

GO when...

  • Grass is only slightly damp.
  • The soil is firm.
  • No more rain is expected.
  • You can mow high and slowly.

CAUTION when...

  • Morning dew is still present.
  • The mower begins to clump.
  • Shaded areas are still wet.
  • There is more rain later.

NO-GO when...

  • Grass is soaked.
  • The soil is muddy or soft.
  • The mower leaves tracks or clumps.
  • There is active rain or storms.

FAQs

Is it bad to mow wet grass?

Yes, it can clog the mower, tear grass and leave an uneven finish.

Can I mow damp grass?

Light dampness may be manageable, but wait if the ground is soft or the mower clumps.

Why should you not mow wet grass?

Wet blades bend and stick together, so the mower cuts poorly.

Can mowing wet grass damage the lawn?

It can damage the look of the lawn and may leave ruts in soft ground.

How do I know if it is too wet?

Walk on it. If your shoes get wet or the ground feels soft, wait.
