Quick answer

A good grilling day needs a dry window, comfortable temperatures and manageable wind. Storms and strong wind are the biggest problems.

GO when...

  • There is a dry lunch or evening window.
  • Wind is manageable.
  • Temperatures are comfortable.
  • Storm risk is low.

CAUTION when...

  • Showers are possible.
  • Wind may affect cooking or smoke.
  • It may cool down later.
  • You may need a safe covered area.

NO-GO when...

  • Rain or thunderstorms are likely.
  • Wind is strong enough to make grilling difficult.
  • Lightning risk exists.
  • Outdoor cooking would be unsafe.

FAQs

Can I grill in light rain?

Only if you have a safe outdoor setup and conditions are mild. Never grill in an enclosed space.

How windy is too windy for grilling?

If wind affects flame control or makes cooking unsafe, wait.

Is thunderstorm risk a NO-GO?

Yes. Lightning and severe weather are a NO-GO.

Does temperature matter?

Yes, because comfort and cooling food can both be affected.
