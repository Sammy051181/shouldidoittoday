Simple answer. Clear reasoning.

The site is built around one question: is today suitable for the thing you want to do?

1. Choose the task

Mowing, washing, drying, painting, BBQs and exercise all need different weather conditions. Each task has its own rule set.

2. Check local weather

Rain risk, wind, temperature and recent rainfall are checked for your location to produce a practical go/no-go answer.

3. Get the SITREP

The answer is shown as GO, CAUTION or NO-GO, with a short explanation and the best time window for today.

Weather-based answers for everyday jobs

Should I Do It Today? is designed for quick everyday decisions. Instead of reading a full weather forecast and guessing what it means, you get a task-specific answer for your location.

Should I mow the lawn today?

The lawn mowing check looks for rain, recent rainfall, wind and suitable temperature. If the grass is likely to be wet, the answer may be caution or no-go even if it is not raining right now.

Should I wash my car today?

The car washing check looks for rain risk, wind and temperature. A dry, calm day is better because heavy wind can blow dirt back onto the car and rain can undo the job.

Can I hang washing out today?

The washing line check looks for low rain risk, enough drying time, useful breeze and reasonable temperature. A windy but dry day can be better than a warm still day.

Should I paint outside today?

The outside painting check is stricter. It looks for dry conditions, low rain risk, moderate temperature and manageable wind. If rain is likely soon, the safest answer is usually no-go.

Should I BBQ today?

The BBQ check balances rain, wind and comfort. A dry day with light or moderate wind usually scores well. Strong wind or heavy rain risk lowers the score quickly.

FAQ

Useful answers for people searching before they start a job outside.

Is this a weather forecast website?

No. It uses weather data, but the point is to give a simple task decision rather than a full forecast.

Does it work in the UK and other English-speaking countries?

Yes. You can search for towns and cities in the UK, Ireland, United States, Canada, Australia, New Zealand and other locations supported by the weather data provider.

Why does it say caution instead of yes or no?

Caution means the task is possible, but conditions are not ideal. For example, it may be dry now but rain could arrive later, or wind may be stronger than ideal.

Can I trust the answer completely?

No forecast-based tool can guarantee local conditions. Use it as a guide and check the actual weather where you are before starting.
