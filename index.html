    What the answer means

    GO means the weather looks suitable. CAUTION means it may be possible, but there is a practical issue to check. NO-GO means waiting is likely the better choice.

    The checker uses local forecast data, recent rain, daylight, wind, temperature and task-specific common-sense rules.

    Limitations

    This is practical guidance, not a guarantee. Always check the actual conditions where you are, especially for storms, ice, heat, tools, ladders, pets and outdoor cooking.
