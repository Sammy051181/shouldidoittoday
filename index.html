These are the everyday decisions the checker is built around.

Should I cut the grass today? Can I hang washing out today? Should I water the plants today? Should I BBQ today? Should I go for a run today? Do I need a coat today?

What the answer means

GO means the weather looks suitable. CAUTION means it may be possible, but there is a practical issue to check. NO-GO means waiting is probably the better choice.

The checker uses local forecast data, recent rain, daylight, wind, temperature and task-specific common-sense rules.

How it works

  • Enter a town, city or postcode.
  • Choose the job you are considering.
  • Pick today, tomorrow or the weekend.
  • Get a short decision, best window and reasons.

Limitations

This is practical weather guidance, not a guarantee. Always check the actual conditions where you are, especially for storms, ice, heat, tools, ladders, pets and outdoor cooking.
