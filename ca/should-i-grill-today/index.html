Should I grill today in Canada?

Check local weather conditions before firing up the grill — rain, wind, temperature and storm risk all matter.

Check your local conditions

GO when...

  • Rain risk is low during your cooking window.
  • Wind is manageable for the grill.
  • Temperature is comfortable for outdoor cooking.
  • No storm or thunder risk.

CAUTION when...

  • Afternoon showers possible — have a covered area nearby.
  • Cool temperatures — dress warmly and protect the food.
  • Wind may affect grill temperature and control.
  • Evening cooldown may arrive faster than expected.

NO-GO when...

  • Thunderstorms or lightning are forecast.
  • Very strong wind gusts make grilling unsafe or impractical.
  • Heavy rain or hail is expected.
  • Extreme cold makes the grill hard to heat and food cools quickly.

Frequently asked questions

Can I grill in cold weather?

Yes, with adjustments. Cold air means the grill takes longer to heat and food cools faster. Add extra cooking time and keep the lid closed more often.

What wind speed is too strong for grilling?

Gusts above about 30 km/h can make it hard to keep the grill at a stable temperature and blow flames unpredictably. Strong gusts are a safety concern.

Can I grill in light rain?

Light drizzle is manageable with a covered patio or BBQ canopy. Heavier rain or thunder means cooking indoors is the safer choice.

How do I grill safely in autumn or shoulder seasons?

Check the forecast carefully, have a sheltered spot, and avoid grilling if thunderstorm warnings are active.
